Game protagonist concepts (part 1)

Exploring character concept sketches in the service of a well-meaning bloke I happen to call a close friend
date published
February 19, 2020

A bolt from the blue

A very good friend of mine recently approached me with an irrefusable proposition:

“Wanna see something truly ugly?”

Of course!

He shared with me a rather intriguing video capture of some (absolutely stunning) stick-figure art walking around a matrix of flat blocks while dodging a series of shape primitives randomly moving about the map. Intriguing in the sense that any time i see a fledgeling game prototype I instantly morph into giddy toddler mode.

As captivating to an audience as stick figure art can be, I found myself unable to restrain my urge to scribble up some character concepts to give the stickman protagonist a tad more form. Suppressing my instinct to draw whatever it was I wanted and hijack the concept entirely, I opted to ask more details about the concept he had in mind.

It turns out to be quite an interesting one. Without giving too many of his prized ideas away, the concept involves stealth action set in a dystopian, technologically-advanced world. Think hacking panels, terminals, and other devices. Evading surveillance, thugs, traps, and who knows what else.

Initial Conceptualization

My initial intuition in this setting is to ditch the usual go-to archetype in this scenario, and angle towards a more flashy, confident female character. One full of bravado, sarcasm, but with a hint of class and refinement. I also realized acutely just how long it had been since attempting to conceptualize a game character. Though, with a bit of grit and an unusual amount of uninterrupted time, I was able to produce this initial sketch:

It was a fun exercise that portrayed some of my initial thoughts.

I diligently forwarded my first sketch (in far too long) over to Dave for his feedback, which read something along these lines:

“I was actually imagining it more to be gender non-specific so that anyone could project themselves into it”

Important note to remember. Ask your clients first about their expectations., even if they are close friends who technically never solicited your assistance to begin with.

No bother. I never expected to satisfy anyone on my first rough concept in far too long. I forged ahead with a character that could more ambiguously signal their gender. Some form of utility coat felt in order. Perhaps the character more gangly in stature, allowing cloth to fill out their form to add much needed volume (and visual interest).

Much more satisfying for my tastes, though certainly not perfect and in need of more detailed conceptualization. This character embodies more mystery and intrigue than the previously conceptualized strong female archetype. Quite excited to hear the feedback on this one.

Which read something like this:

“I didn't have a crystal clear vision of it yet, but what I was vaguely imagining was a thief in a minimalist tactical suit that is form-fitting so that they make less noise, maybe some gear pouches but not super visible. face mask over nose and mouth with eyes visible and a shock of colored hair on top, maybe slightly chunky-spiky like Chrono Trigger hair”

Spiraling around specifics

At this point the direction seemed pretty clear. Dave had a pretty strong vision for the character in his head afterall – which is usually a good thing! I prefer having to hit a vision rather than circling around nebulous feedback that has no aim other than to be critical.

I took pen to canvas, keeping the accrued points of feedback in mind, in an attempt to drill down to a concept that closely matched Dave's vision.

Lo and behold, I just wound up conceptualizing Kakashi from Naruto

Clearly not a concept that would work for a game, given its likeness to a famous character in one of the most popular anime series ever produced.

Besides, there are other issues illustrated by the kakashi-clone concept which don't exactly work with Dave's earlier points, such as gender ambiguity or desired genre.

Undaunted by the failure, I attempted a slightly different concept of masking the face of the player entirely. Goggles, I thought to myself. Goggles are in sharp order here! Carry over some of the more cargo-like fatigues one might find on a typical jounin and perhaps we are in business.

The earlier sketch iteration for this character was more fluid, and did not have a cowl. My inspiration had stemmed from a mash-up of concepts ranging from hunter classes found in a certain popular MMRPG game, mixed with a bit of space horror suit design, with some utility straps and belts added for good measure.

Defeated but not discouraged

If I am being honest, it would be clear to observe the progression at this point appears to be plagued by fatigue. It all felt wrong. I was honing in too closely to detail, losing focus of what makes an original character interesting.

I dutifully forwarded the sketches along anyway, noting my dissatisfaction with the ongoing progress. While Dave seemed to agree with my self critique and assessment, he was gracious enough to show continued interest and support.

It was a fun opportunity to help explore some quick ideas in the game space again, even with some scratchy sketch work that I would doubtfully find publish-worthy at a certain time in my art production career days.

There is always tomorrow and the inspiration the day may bring!
